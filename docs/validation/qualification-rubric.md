# Design Partner Qualification Rubric

Use this rubric after every interview.

Score each company from 0-3 for every category.

## Scores

0 means no signal.

1 means weak signal.

2 means clear signal.

3 means strong signal with concrete evidence.

## Categories

### Stablecoin Usage

- 0: Does not use stablecoins.
- 1: Has tested stablecoins but no recurring usage.
- 2: Uses stablecoins monthly for business activity.
- 3: Uses stablecoins weekly or daily for customer, vendor, or treasury activity.

### Reconciliation Pain

- 0: No reconciliation problem.
- 1: Mild annoyance.
- 2: Recurring manual work or uncertainty.
- 3: Frequent, costly, or high-risk reconciliation pain.

### Matching Ambiguity

- 0: Payments are always obvious.
- 1: Occasional uncertainty.
- 2: Multiple examples of hard-to-match transactions.
- 3: Matching ambiguity causes support, fulfillment, or finance problems.

### Exception Pain

- 0: No underpaid, overpaid, late, wrong-token, wrong-chain, or orphan cases.
- 1: Rare exceptions.
- 2: Exceptions happen and require manual resolution.
- 3: Exceptions are a major operational burden.

### Current Workaround Weakness

- 0: Current system works well.
- 1: Some manual steps.
- 2: Wallet explorers, spreadsheets, custom scripts, or manual Slack updates are common.
- 3: Current workaround is fragile, slow, or blocks scale.

### Buyer Urgency

- 0: Curious only.
- 1: Would look later.
- 2: Wants a solution this quarter.
- 3: Has active pain and wants to test quickly.

### MVP Fit

- 0: Needs a different product.
- 1: Needs many features outside v1.
- 2: Fits the v1 workflow with minor gaps.
- 3: The v1 workflow maps directly to their current pain.

### Trust And Access

- 0: Cannot test or share workflow.
- 1: Can discuss but cannot provide examples.
- 2: Can share examples and test in sandbox.
- 3: Can test with real or production-like transactions.

## Qualification Bands

Strong design partner:

- Total score: 18-24
- Must include 2+ for stablecoin usage, reconciliation pain, and MVP fit.

Possible design partner:

- Total score: 12-17
- Good to keep warm, but needs more proof.

Not a design partner:

- Total score: 0-11
- Useful learning, but not enough urgency for MVP validation.

## Red Flags

- They mainly want custody.
- They mainly want off-ramp or FX.
- They mainly want cards.
- They mainly want full accounting or tax reporting.
- They do not have real stablecoin volume.
- They cannot name a current owner of the workflow.
- They like the idea but will not commit to testing.

## Design Partner Commitment

A qualified design partner should agree to:

- Share current workflow details.
- Provide at least one real pain example.
- Test the MVP.
- Give weekly feedback during beta.
- Let Twins observe whether the product reduces manual work.
